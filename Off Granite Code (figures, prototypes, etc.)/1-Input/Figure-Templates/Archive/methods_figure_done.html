Description
Variable
Number of patients ($w$ = white, $p$ = people of color).
$n_w$, $n_p$
List containing the first day of hospice for those patients who received hospice care
$X_w$, $X_p$
Number of patients who received hospice care. ($u_w = |X_w|$, $u_p = |X_p|$)
$u_w$, $u_p$
Utilization Data Matrices: Matrices where each row represents patient $i$ and each column represents day $j \in [200]$. The entry $i,j$ is 1 if patient $i$ had received hospice by day $j$.
$\mathbf{W}_{i,j}$, $\mathbf{P}_{i,j}$
The difference Signal: For a given day $j \in [200]$ the number of white patients who received hospice by day $j$ minus the number of patients of color who received hospice by day $j$.
{#
$\textbf{diffSignal}(\mathbf{W}, \mathbf{P})_j$
#}
$\textbf{diffSignal}_j$
White More / POC More Probability of Clinically Significant Difference: The probability that the difference between the fraction of white patients and patients of color who received hospice by day $j$ is greater than $0.05$ favoring white patients and patients of color respectively.
$\textbf{WMPCD}_j,$
$\textbf{PMPCD}_j$
Day By Day Fisher Exact test p-value series. The p-value of a fisher exact test comparing the number of white patients and patients of color who received hospice by day $j$. Defined for each $j \in [200]$.
$\textbf{DBDFE}_j$
Test Null Hypothesis
Test Description
Lvl.
$$\frac{u_w}{n_w} = \frac{u_p}{n_p}$$
Fisher Exact Test ($\textbf{FE}$)
Represents the quality measures approach. The null hypothesis is that the fraction of white patients and patients of color who received hospice care is the same. Positive if $p < 0.05$, denoted $\textbf{FE}(+)$, otherwise $\textbf{FE}(-)$.
Hospital
$X_w$ and $X_p$ have the same mean.
Mann-Whitney U Test ($\textbf{MW}$)
Represents a simple comparison that takes into account the timing of the first day of hospice. The null hypothesis is that the first day of hospice for white patients and patients of color have the same mean. Positive if $p < 0.05$, denoted $\textbf{MW}(+)$, otherwise $\textbf{MW}(-)$.
Hospital
For a given day $j \in [200]$: $\textbf{PMPCD}_j < 0.95$
Test for Probable Clinically Significant Difference (with Patients of Color receiving More hospice)
Positive if $\textbf{PMPCD}_j \ge 0.95$, denoted $\textbf{PMPCD}_j(+)$. $\textbf{PMPCD}_j(-)$ otherwise.
Day
For a given day $j \in [200]$: $\textbf{WMPCD}_j < 0.95$
Test for Probable Clinically Significant Difference (with White patients receiving More hospice)
Positive if $\textbf{WMPCD}_j \ge 0.95$, denoted $\textbf{WMPCD}_j(+)$. $\textbf{WMPCD}_j(-)$ otherwise.
Day
For a given day $j \in [200]$, neither $\textbf{PMPCD}_j$ nor $\textbf{WMPCD}_j$ are greater than $0.95$.
Test for Probable Clinically Significant Difference
Positive if either $\textbf{PMPCD}_j(+)$ or $\textbf{WMPCD}_j(+)$. Denoted $\textbf{PCD}_j(+)$ if positive and $\textbf{PCD}_j(-)$ otherwise.
Day
For a given day $j \in [200]$: $$\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n_w}{\mathbf{W}_{i,j}}}{n_w} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n_p}{\mathbf{P}_{i,j}}}{n_p}$$
Day By Day Fisher Exact Test
Positive for a given day $j$ if $\textbf{DBDFE}_j < 0.05$, denoted $\textbf{DBDFE}_j(+)$. $\textbf{DBDFE}_j(-)$ otherwise.
